# User Guide

Welcome to the Online Examination Platform User Guide. This document provides instructions for Students and Instructors/Administrators on how to use the platform.

## For Students

### 1. Getting Started
- **Registration**: Navigate to the registration page (`/register.html`) to create your account.
- **Login**: Use your credentials to log in (`/login.html`).

### 2. Dashboard
- Upon logging in, you will see your **Student Dashboard**.
- This dashboard displays a list of **Available Exams** that you are eligible to take.
- You can also see your **Recent Results** from past exams.

### 3. Taking an Exam
- Click "Start" on an exam card to begin.
- **Rules**:
  - The timer will start immediately.
  - Do not switch tabs or windows, as this may be recorded as suspicious behavior.
  - Answer all questions before the time runs out.
- **Submission**: Click "Submit" when finished. If time runs out, the exam may auto-submit.

### 4. Viewing Results
- After submission (depending on exam settings), you may see your score immediately.
- Go to the **Results** page (`/results.html`) to view detailed performance history.

## For Instructors & Administrators

### 1. Dashboard Overview
- The **Instructor Dashboard** (`/admin.html`) gives you a quick overview of system activity.
- Navigation links allow access to Exams, Users, Analytics, and Grading.

### 2. Managing Exams
- Navigate to **Exams** (`/admin-exams.html`).
- **Create Exam**: Click "Create New Exam". Fill in details like Title, Duration, and Schedule.
- **Add Questions**: You can select questions from the Question Bank or create new ones directly.
- **Edit/Delete**: Use the actions on the exam list to modify existing exams.

### 3. Monitoring Live Exams
- Use the **Live Monitor** (`/admin-monitor.html`) to see students currently taking exams.
- You can track progress and status in real-time.

### 4. Grading
- For objective questions (True/False, MCQ), grading is automatic.
- For subjective questions (Short Answer), navigate to **Grading** (`/grading.html`).
- Review student answers and assign scores manually.

### 5. Analytics
- The **Analytics Dashboard** (`/admin-analytics.html`) provides visual insights.
- View charts for Pass/Fail rates, Average Scores, and other metrics.

### 6. User Management
- Manage students and other instructors via the **Users** board (`/admin-users.html`).
- You can View, Edit, or Deactivate users as needed.
